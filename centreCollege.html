Centre College's True Colors

Private || Liberal Arts || 4-Year || SACSCOC Accredited

Overall Rating:

Policies and Procedures

In-progress and developed, student awareness of, and ease of finding policies and procedures. Defined process for changing or updating preferred name, pronouns, and/or gender identity.

Events and Programming

Availability, quality, and frequency of convocations, presentations, Pride celebrations, courses and majors related to gender and sexual identity.

Student Facilitated

Organizations, groups, and clubs that build relationships and strengthen the LGBTQ+ student community.

Faculty and/or Staff Facilitated

Committees, organizations, groups, and clubs that foster collaboration and cooperation between faculty, staff, and students to celebration and strengthen the LGBTQ+ community on campus.

Available Resources

Including Title IX, counseling, safe/dedicated spaces, easily identified allies, and a central location or webpage dedicated to cataloging resources.

Campus Culture

These are the intangibles; the feeling of inclusion and visibility of LGBTQ+ Pride seen and experienced around campus on any given day.

Safety

Frequency of incidents of hate speech, or violence toward members of the LGBTQ+ community on campus and in the surrounding area. Swiftness and effectiveness campus safety or law enforcement response if/when incidents occur. Feeling of safety on the average day or night out on campus or in town.

Current Students and Alumni Reviews

Class of 2024

"Centre is much more inclusive than my high school and family. I feel like I can be myself on campus and am excited to participate in the campus Pride event."

Class of 2023

"Centre College is very inclusive and welcoming. We have a Pride Alliance which is student-led, and the faculty in the community are very visible. There is a Pride Space that is secured by a coded lock and is not widely advertised to the general campus community. Overall a great place to be!"

Class of 2022

"Centre is making progress towards inclusivity, acceptance, and equality. It is better than what it was as an incoming freshman, but there is still room for improvement."

Class of 2021

"Centre College was inclusive on paper, but outward support on campus was lacking. I have gotten comments from other students and professors about my sexuality, and my gender identity is not respected."
