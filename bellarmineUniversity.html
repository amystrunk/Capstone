Policies and Procedures

Events and Programming

Availability, quality, and frequency of convocations, presentations, Pride celebrations, courses and majors related to gender and sexual identity.

Student Facilitated

Organizations, groups, and clubs that build relationships and strengthen the LGBTQ+ student community.

Faculty and/or Staff Facilitated

Committees, organizations, groups, and clubs that foster collaboration and cooperation between faculty, staff, and students to celebration and strengthen the LGBTQ+ community on campus.

Available Resources

Including Title IX, counseling, safe/dedicated spaces, easily identified allies, and a central location or webpage dedicated to cataloging resources.

Campus Culture

These are the intangibles; the feeling of inclusion and visibility of LGBTQ+ Pride seen and experienced around campus on any given day.

Safety

Frequency of incidents of hate speech, or violence toward members of the LGBTQ+ community on campus and in the surrounding area. Swiftness and effectiveness campus safety or law enforcement response if/when incidents occur. Feeling of safety on the average day or night out on campus or in town.

Current Students and Alumni Reviews

Class of 2024

"Bellarmine University is a more freeing place than high school was, and offers many more opportunities to engage with other lgbtq+ students. We have various alliance groups and events tailored toward our community that were never present or possible in other settings, so it allows us to feel more included and a higher sense of belonging. Even with these, however, there is always room for improvement and more things available for us, and I hope we are able to keep moving in better directions."

Class of 2023

"Bellarmine University hates gay people. We do not have sexual orientation or gender identity in our nondiscrimination policy, and professors love to out gay people to the rest of campus. I wish I hadn’t gone here, 0/10 do not recommend."

Class of 2022

"BU is making progress towards inclusivity, acceptance, and equality. It is better than what it was as an incoming freshman, but there is still room for improvement."

Class of 2021

"Bellarmine was inclusive on paper, but outward support on campus was lacking. I have gotten comments from other students and professors about my sexuality, and my gender identity is not respected."
